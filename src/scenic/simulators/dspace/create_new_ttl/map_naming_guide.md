Laguna Seca is a **numbered-corner track** (Turns 1–11), with a few iconic sections that have proper names. Here’s how it’s structured:

---

# 🏁 Main Sections (Start/Finish & Straights)

* **Front Straight** – Start/finish line; uphill into Turn 1
* **Pit Straight** – Sometimes used interchangeably with front straight
* **Rahal Straight** – Back straight between Turn 6 and the Corkscrew (named after Bobby Rahal)

---

# 🌀 Numbered Corners (Turns 1–11)

### **Turn 1**

* Fast uphill kink
* Often flat-out in high-downforce cars

### **Turn 2 – Andretti Hairpin**

* Double-apex left hairpin
* Heavy braking zone
* Major overtaking spot

### **Turn 3**

* Medium-speed right

### **Turn 4**

* Medium-speed left

### **Turn 5**

* Left-hander leading uphill

### **Turn 6**

* Fast uphill left, sets up Rahal Straight

---

# 🎢 The Iconic Section

### **Turn 7/8/8A – The Corkscrew**

* Technically:

  * **Turn 7** = fast left before braking
  * **Turn 8** = blind left entry
  * **Turn 8A** = right drop
* Massive elevation drop (~59 ft / 18 m in seconds)
* One of the most famous corners in motorsport

---

### **Turn 9 – Rainey Curve**

* Fast sweeping left
* Off-camber
* Named after Wayne Rainey

### **Turn 10**

* Very fast right-hand bend
* Commitment corner

### **Turn 11**

* Slow hairpin
* Leads back to front straight
* Prime overtaking spot

---

# 📐 Naming Convention Summary

Laguna Seca uses:

* **Turn numbers (1–11)** for official layout
* **Hairpin** for tight 180° corners
* **Curve** for high-speed sweepers
* **Straight** for long acceleration zones
* Special names for iconic corners (Andretti Hairpin, Corkscrew, Rainey Curve)
